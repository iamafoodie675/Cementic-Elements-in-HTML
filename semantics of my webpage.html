Things you may not know about cats

While cats don't speak like humans, they develop a unique set of vocalizations specifically for communicating with their owners. They don’t usually meow at other cats, but they use meowing as a way to communicate with humans. Each cat can have its own set of "words" for specific needs, like asking for food, affection, or attention.

Unlike dogs, who have a limited range of vocalizations, cats can produce more than 100 distinct sounds. From meows, purrs, and chirps to growls and hisses, each sound can convey a different emotion or need.

A cat’s whiskers are not just for measuring gaps and spaces—they’re incredibly sensitive to touch. The whiskers help cats sense changes in their surroundings and detect prey in the dark, and they even help them measure the width of spaces before attempting to fit through them.
